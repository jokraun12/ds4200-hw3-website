Welcome to the second page of my personal website! Here, I will go into my previously described passion in more detail. As mentioned, I always had a passion for boats, but never owned one myself. I was extremely lucky to have friends that had boats, which made it possible for me to experience such a cool thing at a younger age than most, and this is also what led to me developing this passion in the first place.

          The way Freedom Boat Club operates is similar to a “country club.” Customers can pay a monthly membership fee and in return, they can reserve our boats for free, and they only pay for the gas that they use. Therefore, our company owns fleets of boats, and it is our job to maintain them, gas them up, clean them, and ensure they are operating properly. There happens to be Freedom locations all over the Boston area – Charlestown, Hingham, Quincy, Plymouth, and Scituate just to name a few, so I had the opportunity to take boats all around Boston Harbor for employee duties. As I’m sure you could imagine, this was very similar to my previous job at a restaurant when it came to customer service skills. Dealing with customers, especially unhappy customers, is a very important skill, and while it was a much different customer-base at the boat club, there were a lot of similarities there.

Things I learned on the job:

  • Fix many common issues on boats regarding the navigation system, the engine, the water pump, and the electrical wiring.
  • Get a boat on a trailer at the ramp, as well as launch a boat off of a trailer (my favorite one).
  • Perform an oil change on an out-board boat engine!
  • How to tow a boat that was out of service using another similarly sized boat.
  • To tie a boat down to a kleat on the docks.
